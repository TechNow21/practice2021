Example 4

Lab 4

Brief Instructions:

Combining Images with a mask

The objective of this project was to use two images, and combine part or parts of one into the other to create a combined image. In this project, I took the butterflies from the second picture and combined them in the second picture with a mask.

I chose to do it this way because I wanted to learn how to combine certain images in totally different environment.

What I did:

1. Opened Butterflies image in Photoshop.

2. To add the Wolves image used Drag and Drop; then scaled the image to cover the canvas completely

3. Selected the Wolves layer in the layers panel and added a layer mask by clicking add layer mask icon.

4. To mask the image: Used brush tool - see the brush size to 55 and hardness 20 to make soft edges. Painted with white to reveal and black to conceal as needed to reveal the butterflies.

5. For Outer Glow: Selected Wolves layer - Right Clicked on Blending Options - In the Layer Style Selected Outer Glow - Blend Mode = Overlay; Opacity = 59%; Noise 0%; Gradient = Neutral, Off-White 01; Technique = Softer; Spread = 0; Size 43 px; Quality: Range = 62; Jitter = 10.

6. Saved and Exported image for web friendly version.
